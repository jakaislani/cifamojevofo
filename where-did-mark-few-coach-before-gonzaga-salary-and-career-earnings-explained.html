Following the athletic career, Mark Few is an American college basketball coach who has been the head coach at Gonzaga since 1999. Here is everything about his personal life.

Mark Few whose real name is Mark Norman Few is an American college basketball coach who has been the head coach at Gonzaga University since 1999.

Therefore, he has served on Gonzaga’s coaching staff since 1989 and has been a constant on the sidelines throughout a period that has seen the Bulldogs rise from mid-major obscurity to consistent NCAA Tournament contenders.

During his tenure as head coach, Few has led the Bulldogs to the NCAA Tournaments every season when the team had secured an automatic bid but the tournament was canceled.

Where Did Mark Few Coach Before Gonzaga?

Before Gonzaga, Mark Few has also worked at Oregon’s summer basketball camps, as an assistant, Few helped lead Gonzaga to its first four postseason appearances in 1994, 1996, and 1998 National Invitation Tournaments.

At the time of writing this article, there is no exact information about what he did before Gonzaga, all we know about him is related to Gonzaga only.

He entered the coaching profession even before receiving his degree, serving as an unpaid part-time assistant at his alma mater of Crewell High School.

After a season as an assistant at another Oregon school, Sheldon High School in Eugene, he moved to Spokane, Washington, joining the Gonzaga staff as a graduate assistant in 1989 under Dan Fitgerald.

He had some familiarity with the program, as he had befriended Dan Monson, then a Gonzaga assistant and later the head coach, during his time working the Oregan basketball camps.
