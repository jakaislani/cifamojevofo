    How To Serve Chili

    Need some ideas on how to serve this chili? It’s obviously fantastic on it’s own but mix it up with these tasty ideas:

  • Serve with our favorite dinner rolls, biscuits, cornbread or this tasty beer bread.
  • Fabulous served up in a nacho bar.
  • On top of baked potatoes or baked sweet potatoes.
  • Chili cheese hot dogs or hamburgers are always a crowd pleaser.
  • Serve with warmed tortillas. Chris’s mom warms up the tortillas, butters them and rolls them like taquitos. Slice them in half and then just stack them high and serve alongside the chili.

    • Turkey Chili Ingredients

    • ground turkey – use the 93% lean turkey for the best flavor
    • extra virgin olive oil
    • onion and garlic: an amazing flavor base combination
    • spices and seasonings: chili powder, smoked paprika, ground cumin, salt, pepper, cayenne pepper, ground cinnamon, oregano, bay leaf
    • brown sugar: the sugar balances the acidity of the tomatoes, it does not make the chili sweet.
    • chicken broth or stock: look for the low sodium variety
    • diced and crushed tomatoes: the entire can goes in, juice and all
    • black beans: make sure to drain the beans and then rinse thoroughly. Pinto beans or kidney beans can be used instead and or in addition to the black beans. You can also double up on the beans to stretch the chili. Add an additional 1 cup of chicken broth if you double the beans.

    How To Make This Easy Turkey Chili

  • Brown the ground turkey and then add the onion and cook until translucent. Stir in garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  • Add the chili powder, cumin, cinnamon, salt, and pepper and stir to combine.
  • Add crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, remaining spices and bay leaf and the chicken broth. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  • Stir in the black beans and simmer chili for another 5 minutes or so.
  • Serve with your favorite toppings! See my suggestions below.

    • Chili Topping Options

    The sky is the limit when it comes to chili toppings. This turkey chili is absolutely amazing on it’s own but we are not ones to resist making a good thing better.

    Here are some of our favorite toppings for this turkey chili recipe:

    • shredded cheese – sharp cheddar is my favorite but pepper jack would be delicious too
    • corn chips or tortilla chips – crunchy and salty, it’s a win win
    • avocados – slices or chunks of fresh avocado add a lovely coolness and creaminess
    • sour cream – a delicious way to cool down the turkey and add more creamy flavor
    • jalapeños – pickled or fresh are both delicious
    • green onions – adds a bright, fresh flavor
    • cilantro – the same as green onions, and my personal favorite

    Turkey Chili FAQs

    Can turkey chili be frozen?

    Yes, it sure can. Let the chili cool completely and then transfer to freezer bag or airtight container. The chili can be frozen for up to 3 months. Let the chili thaw overnight in the refrigerator and then reheat in the microwave or on the stove top.

    How should I store leftovers?

    Leftovers should be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

    Can this chili be made in advance?

    Yes! Because this recipe is actually better the second day, I often make the chili the day before I plan to serve it. It may need additional chicken broth when reheating to thin it out.

    Can this recipe be made in the slow cooker?

    It can! You can check out my crockpot chili recipe for inspiration as well. Simply follow steps 1 and 2 in the recipe card below. Transfer the turkey mixture and all of the other ingredients, except for the black beans, to the slow cooker. Cook on low for 6 to 8 hours. Stir in the black beans, cover and cook an additional 30 minutes then serve.

    Turkey Chili

    This incredibly easy Turkey Chili recipe is delicious, hearty, healthy and has amazing flavor! A perfect blend of spices, ground turkey, tomatoes and beans. Don't forget the toppings!

    Course Main Dish, Soup Cuisine American Keyword turkey chili, turkey chili recipe Prep Time 15 minutes minutes Cook Time 30 minutes minutes Servings 6 Calories 272kcal Author Trish – Mom On Timeout

    Ingredients

    • 1 pound ground turkey
    • 1 to 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
    • 1 onion large, chopped
    • 2 garlic cloves minced
    • 1/4 cup chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
    • 14 ounces crushed tomatoes juice and all
    • 14 ounces diced tomatoes juice and all
    • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
    • 1 teaspoon oregano
    • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper optional
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1 cup chicken broth or stock low sodium
    • 14 ounces black beans drained and thoroughly rinsed

    Instructions

    • In a large, deep skillet, dutch oven, or pot, add 1 to 2 tablespoons of olive oil and ground turkey. Brown the meat until it is no longer pink, about 4 to 6 minutes.

    • Add the chopped onions and cook, stirring frequently, until translucent, about 3 to 4 minutes. Add garlic and cook one minute longer, stirring frequently.

    • Add the chili powder, cumin, cinnamon, salt, pepper smoked paprika and stir to combine letting spices cook for a minute or so.

    • Add crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, brown sugar, oregano, cayenne, bay leaf and chicken broth. Stir to combine, reduce heat to medium low and simmer for 15 minutes.

    • Add the black beans, stirring to combine and simmer chili for another 5 minutes or so. Add additional chicken broth to thin out chili if desired. Serve immediately with desired toppings.
